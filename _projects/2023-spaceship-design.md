This project presents a fluid-mechanical dissection of a pneumatic air grinder, with the goal of connecting its internal hardware to core fluid mechanics principles. We analyzed how compressed air enters the tool, is regulated by the throttle system, and ultimately drives a pneumatic vane motor to produce high-speed rotational motion.
