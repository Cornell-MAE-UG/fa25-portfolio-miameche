## Overview

This project is based on a homework assignment from my heat transfer course. The assignment involved analyzing heat transfer in extended surfaces (fins), building thermal resistance networks for a heat sink system, and studying the transient heating of a wire using the lumped capacitance method.

## Reflection

I found one assignment from my MAE3240 Heat Transfer class especially valuable, which was a problem set involving fin heat transfer, thermal resistance networks, and transient heating of a wire. Working through the fin analysis provided me an in-depth understanding of how extended surfaces increase heat transfer by increasing available area for convection. The assignment required calculations of temperature distributions, fin efficiency, and heat transfer rates. Building the thermal resistance network for the heat sink system also taught me how to analyze a thermal system by combining conduction, convection, and contact resistance.

The transient heating problem highlighted how the lumped capacitance method works and taught me a systematic approach of analysis. It was important to check the Biot number first to determine if it was reasonable to make an assumption regarding whether the wire had a uniform temperature. I then used an energy balance to derive the temperature as a function of time to connect theory with physical behavior. Overall, this assignment greatly furthered my understanding of both steady-state and transient heat transfer and their real-world engineering applications.
